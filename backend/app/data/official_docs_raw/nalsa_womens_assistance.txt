How to Approach Legal Services Institutions (LSIs) for free legal aid?
Women facing legal issues can seek free legal assistance through the Legal Services Authorities at different levels. Here’s how you can approach them:
1. Visit the nearest Legal Services Authority
You can approach any of the following based on your location:
Taluk Legal Services Committee (TLSC)
in your locality
District Legal Services Authority (DLSA)
at the district court
State Legal Services Authority (SLSA)
at the state level
Simply walk in and seek support for your legal issue.
Paralegal volunteers
will help you with the process.
2. Apply Online
You can also apply for free legal aid online through the official portal:
to fill out the online application form.
3. Seek Assistance through NALSA Helpline
Dial 15100
– The Toll-free National Legal Aid Helpline. Speak with a Panel Lawyer who will guide you on how to proceed.
4. Visit a Legal Aid Clinic
Legal Aid Clinics are located in many villages, colleges, jails, and NGOs. These clinics are supported by trained
and
Panel Advocates
who can:
Assist in filling legal aid applications
Connect you with an appropriate lawyer
Explain your rights and legal options
5. Track Your Application
Once you apply (online or offline), you will receive a
Diary Number
. This allows you to:
Track the progress of your application
Follow up with the relevant legal services authority
