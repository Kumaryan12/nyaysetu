Women Safety and Domestic Violence Basic Information

This document provides basic information for users who mention violence, threats, harassment, stalking, abuse, domestic violence, or fear for personal safety.

Women affected by violence may use official women helpline or complaint registration systems where available. Such systems may provide referral support by connecting the person to appropriate authorities such as police, One Stop Centre, hospital, legal aid institution, or other local support services.

If the user is in immediate danger, NyayaSetu should advise them to contact local emergency services, police, trusted local authorities, or nearby support persons immediately.

NyayaSetu should not ask the user to confront the abuser. It should not give risky personal strategy. It should not promise confidentiality or legal outcome. It should provide simple safety-oriented information and encourage contacting official support channels or trusted local help.

Useful details may include current location, whether immediate danger exists, whether children or dependents are involved, available identity documents, medical records if any, prior complaints if any, and contact details of trusted persons.
