Labour and Unpaid Wages Basic Information

This document provides basic public-service information for workers who have complaints related to non-payment of wages, minimum wages, termination of services, or labour law violations.
A worker facing non-payment of wages should keep records such as appointment letter, employment contract, salary slips, attendance records, bank statements, payment messages, identity card, work assignment records, and written communication with the employer.
The user may contact the appropriate Labour Department or labour grievance authority. Procedure and contact points may vary by state or district.
NyayaSetu should explain this as general public-service information. It should not decide whether a particular employer has violated the law. It should not calculate legal compensation or guarantee the result of a complaint.
For urgent exploitation, threats, violence, or forced labour concerns, users should contact appropriate local authorities, police, or legal aid institutions.
