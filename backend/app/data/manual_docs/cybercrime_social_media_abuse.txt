Cybercrime Social Media Abuse Reporting Information

This document summarizes official-source guidance from the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal page for reporting abuse to social media intermediaries.

The official portal provides links for citizens to report online illegal activity directly to social media intermediaries. The listed platforms include Facebook, Twitter/X, Google, Instagram, Telegram, WhatsApp, YouTube, Public, and Koo.

This source is relevant for issues such as online abuse, blackmail through social media, threats to share private photos or videos, impersonation, harassment, stalking, abusive messages, and misuse of social media accounts.

If someone is threatening to leak private photos or videos, the user should preserve evidence immediately. Useful evidence may include screenshots, profile links, usernames, phone numbers, chat messages, URLs, timestamps, and any payment or extortion demands.

The user may report the matter through the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal and may also use the relevant social media platform reporting channel. If there is immediate danger, physical threat, stalking, extortion, or risk of violence, the user should contact the nearest police station or emergency services.

NyayaSetu should not promise removal of content or guarantee police action. It should guide the user to preserve evidence, report through official channels, and seek urgent help if safety is at risk.
